# Boat Co-Ownership Feature Gathering Interview
## Founder's Guide to Validating & Building the Product

**Purpose**: Validate the co-ownership management app idea and gather feature requirements from real boat co-owners.

**Target**: 20-30 interviews minimum for statistically relevant insights

**Duration**: 20-30 minutes per interview

**Format**: Mix of structured (checkboxes/scales) and open-ended questions

---

## How to Use This Document

### Before the Interview
1. Send this as a **Google Form** or **Typeform** (easier to analyze)
2. OR use this as a **conversation guide** (more depth, but harder to analyze)
3. Always introduce yourself and the purpose clearly
4. Promise to share results and early access if you build this

### During the Interview
- Start with screening questions (make sure they're the right person)
- Let them talk freely but guide back to structure
- Probe on pain points ("tell me more about that...")
- Don't pitch features, discover needs
- Record insights in "Notes" section

### After the Interview
- Fill out your observations immediately
- Tag interesting quotes
- Note if they're a potential beta tester
- Score their enthusiasm level

## DATA ANALYSIS GUIDE

### How to Analyze Results After 20+ Interviews

#### 1. Quantitative Analysis

**Pain Point Severity** (Section 2.2):
- Calculate average scores for each issue
- Identify top 5 pain points (highest averages)
- Look for consensus (low standard deviation = everyone agrees)

**Feature Priority** (Section 3.1):
- Calculate average ranking for each feature
- Top 5 features = your MVP
- Bottom 5 features = nice-to-have or skip

**Feature Interest** (Section 3.2):
- Calculate % of respondents who rated 4-5 ("would use")
- Features with 70%+ interest = must-have
- Features with 30-70% = include if easy
- Features with <30% = skip for MVP

**Pricing** (Section 5.3):
- Calculate median willingness to pay
- Identify sweet spot (where most people cluster)
- Premium price = 75th percentile
- Budget price = 25th percentile

#### 2. Qualitative Analysis

**Top Pain Points** (Section 2.3):
- Group similar responses into themes
- Count frequency of each theme
- Create "pain point personas" (different archetypes)

**Killer Feature** (Section 3.3):
- Look for patterns in what people say
- If 30%+ say the same thing = make it your hook
- This is your value proposition

**Dream Solution** (Section 8.1):
- Mine for feature ideas you didn't think of
- Look for emotional language (what they REALLY want)
- Identify unmet needs

**Quotes** (Section 10.3):
- Tag quotes by theme
- Use in marketing copy
- Include in pitch deck

#### 3. Segmentation

**Segment by**:
- Number of co-owners (2-3 vs 4+)
- Boat value (â‚¬50k vs â‚¬500k)
- Tech comfort level
- Years of co-ownership

**Look for**:
- Do different segments have different needs?
- Should you target a specific segment first?
- Are some segments more willing to pay?

#### 4. Decision Criteria

**Build if**:
- âœ… 70%+ of respondents rate top pain points as 4-5 severity
- âœ… 60%+ would pay â‚¬15+ per month
- âœ… 50%+ are enthusiastic (score 4-5) about the solution
- âœ… 40%+ want to beta test
- âœ… Clear top 3-5 must-have features emerge

**Don't build if**:
- âŒ No consensus on pain points (fragmented needs)
- âŒ Less than 30% willing to pay anything
- âŒ Current free tools are "good enough" for most
- âŒ Feature requests are all over the map (no focus)
- âŒ Low enthusiasm scores (<3 average)

#### 5. MVP Feature Set

**Process**:
1. Take top 5 features from ranking (Section 3.1)
2. Add features with 70%+ interest (Section 3.2)
3. Cross-reference with "Killer Feature" responses (Section 3.3)
4. Validate against top pain points (Sections 2.2 & 2.3)
5. Remove anything complex or low ROI

**Your MVP** = The intersection of:
- High pain point severity
- High feature interest
- Frequently mentioned as "killer feature"
- Technically feasible in 3-4 months
